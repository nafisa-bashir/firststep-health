# FirstStep Health

**Developed by Team Genesis for the Tatweer Hackathon 2026**

## Rural Healthcare Business Launch Assistant

🌐 **Live Demo:** https://firststep-health.vercel.app/

FirstStep Health is a web application that helps aspiring healthcare entrepreneurs in Al Qua'a, Abu Dhabi understand the official steps involved in starting a healthcare related business.

Instead of searching across multiple government websites and interpreting fragmented regulatory information, users answer a short questionnaire and receive a personalized roadmap containing a possible approval pathway, required documents, funding opportunities, estimated startup costs, suggested timelines, immediate next steps, and links to official UAE resources.

The application simplifies the early planning stage while encouraging users to verify every recommendation directly with the relevant government authority.

# Challenge

This project addresses the Tatweer Hackathon challenge by improving access to healthcare entrepreneurship guidance for rural communities.

People living in rural communities such as Al Qua'a often face additional barriers when trying to establish healthcare related services. Although government information is publicly available, it is distributed across multiple agencies, websites, regulatory documents, and funding programmes.

For first time entrepreneurs, understanding where to begin can be difficult.

FirstStep Health brings this information together into one guided experience that helps aspiring entrepreneurs understand the official steps involved in launching a healthcare related business.

---

# The Problem

Starting a healthcare related business in Abu Dhabi requires navigating multiple organizations including:

- Department of Health Abu Dhabi (DOH)
- Abu Dhabi Department of Economic Development (ADDED)
- TAMM
- Khalifa Fund
- UAE Government Portal

Although these resources are publicly available, they are spread across multiple platforms and are not presented as one clear, personalized pathway.

Many aspiring entrepreneurs do not know:

- Which authority to contact first
- Which documents are required
- Which funding opportunities may be available
- What costs to expect
- How long the process may take

This creates unnecessary uncertainty during the earliest stage of launching a business.

---

# Target Users

FirstStep Health is designed for people who already possess healthcare related knowledge or qualifications and want guidance on starting a healthcare business in Abu Dhabi.

Examples include:

- Nurses
- Pharmacists
- First aid trainers
- Healthcare assistants
- Community health workers
- Aspiring healthcare entrepreneurs

The application does not train healthcare professionals, provide legal advice, or replace official government licensing procedures.

---

# Our Solution

The application generates a personalized roadmap using information provided by the user, including:

- Intended healthcare service
- Existing qualifications
- Available budget
- Preferred operating model
- Intended location

The generated roadmap includes:

- Personalized introduction
- Recommended first action
- Explanation of the recommendation
- Possible approval pathway
- Required documents
- Funding opportunities
- Estimated startup costs
- Suggested timeline
- Immediate next steps
- Official government resources
- Important disclaimer

All regulatory information is presented as guidance that should be verified directly with the appropriate authority before taking action.

---

# Design Principles

The application was designed around the following principles:

- Keep guidance simple and easy to understand.
- Organize information from trusted government sources.
- Avoid making legal or licensing decisions for users.
- Encourage verification before any investment or business registration.
- Provide practical next steps rather than overwhelming users with regulations.

# Limitations

FirstStep Health is an informational planning assistant.

It does not:

- Provide legal advice
- Provide medical advice
- Issue business licenses
- Guarantee funding approval
- Replace official government procedures

All licensing, funding, legal, and healthcare requirements should be verified directly with the relevant authorities before any action is taken.
