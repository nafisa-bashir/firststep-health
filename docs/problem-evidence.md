# Problem Evidence

## Why FirstStep Health Exists

FirstStep Health was created to address a practical problem faced by aspiring healthcare entrepreneurs in rural Abu Dhabi.

While government services, licensing information, and funding programmes already exist, they are distributed across multiple agencies and websites. Understanding where to begin, which authority to contact, and which documents are required can be confusing for someone starting a healthcare business for the first time.

Rather than replacing government services, FirstStep Health organizes verified public information into a clear, personalized roadmap that helps users understand the first steps they should take.

---

# Evidence of the Problem

## 1. Rural healthcare access remains a documented challenge

Research published in the *Journal of Health Informatics in Developing Countries* identified barriers to healthcare access in rural areas of the UAE using Hatta Hospital as a case study. The study highlights challenges including travel distance, transportation limitations, and reduced access to local healthcare services.

This demonstrates that healthcare accessibility in rural communities remains an important issue that has been documented in academic literature.

---

## 2. The Abu Dhabi Government recognizes the need

The Abu Dhabi Department of Health Healthcare Strategic Plan identifies improving healthcare services in rural communities as part of its Continuum of Care strategy.

This shows that improving healthcare accessibility is already an official government priority rather than an assumed problem.

FirstStep Health supports this objective by helping qualified healthcare professionals better understand the official process for establishing healthcare related services within Abu Dhabi.

---

## 3. The business setup process is fragmented

Starting a healthcare related business requires interaction with multiple organizations, including:

- Department of Health Abu Dhabi (DOH)
- Abu Dhabi Department of Economic Development (ADDED)
- TAMM
- Khalifa Fund
- UAE Government services

Each organization focuses on a specific part of the process.

As a result, new entrepreneurs often need to search across multiple websites before understanding:

- which authority to contact first
- licensing requirements
- required documents
- funding opportunities
- estimated startup costs
- expected timelines

FirstStep Health combines these resources into one structured roadmap while directing users back to the official government sources for verification.

---

## 4. Licensing requirements can be difficult for first time entrepreneurs

Healthcare businesses operate within a regulated environment.

Official licensing guidance includes multiple approval stages, professional qualification requirements, and supporting documentation.

Although this information is publicly available, it is designed as regulatory documentation rather than step by step guidance for new entrepreneurs.

FirstStep Health simplifies this early planning stage by presenting a possible approval pathway that users can verify directly with the appropriate authority.

---

## 5. Healthcare entrepreneurship continues to grow

Published market research indicates continued growth within the UAE home healthcare sector.

This suggests increasing demand for community healthcare services and highlights the importance of making reliable business guidance more accessible to qualified healthcare professionals interested in serving their communities.

---

# Why This Matters

FirstStep Health does not replace government services.

Instead, it helps aspiring healthcare entrepreneurs understand the official process before they begin investing significant time or money.

By bringing together verified information from multiple trusted sources, the application reduces confusion, improves accessibility, and encourages users to follow official licensing pathways.

---

# Scope

FirstStep Health is designed to support planning only.

The application does not:

- issue licenses
- approve businesses
- provide legal advice
- provide medical advice
- guarantee funding approval

Users are encouraged to verify every recommendation directly with the relevant government authority before taking action.
